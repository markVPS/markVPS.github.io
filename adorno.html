Theodor Adorno

Laying down the framework for Critical Theory.

Theodor Ludwig Wiesengrund Adorno (September 11, 1903 - August 6, 1969) was a philosopher, sociologist, psychologist, musicologist, and composer of the at the Frankfurt School of Goethe University Frankfurt. Although there are several teachers from the Frankfurt School that laid the groundwork for future sociologists and philosophers of the Post-War Era, Adorno remains one of the most discussed. The most prominent philosophy to come out of the school, a philosophy Adorno directly contributed to, was Critical Theory.

Dialectic of Enlightenment is often considred Adorno's magnum opus, with one of the most discussed concepts from this text being the culture industry. Adorno and Max Horkheimer (1895–1973) compare pop culture media to items made in a factory line, whose message, or lack their of, is meant to pacify revolutionary tendencies from the masses.
