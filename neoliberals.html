Ronald Reagan & Margaret Thatcher

The embodiments of Neoliberalism.

Ronald Wilson Reagan (February 6, 1911 - June 5, 2004) was the 40th President (1981-1989) of the United States of America. His deregulatory policy changes, often categorized as neoliberal, make him noticable from previous presidents as they allowed more privatization of corporations than any point in Post-War America. This was a drastic and controversial shift in relations between markets and regulations, some would say still affect us today.

Margaret Hilda Thatcher (October 13, 1925 - April 8, 2013) was Prime Minister (1979-1990) of the United Kingdom. She, like Reagan, is known for her neoliberal policy changes, specifially privatizing previously state-owned companies and staunchly opposing trade unions.

Though Neoliberalism has become a somewhat ambiguous term recently, it is generally known for privatization, deregulation, globalization, and reducing government spending. Reagan and Thatcher are not the first neoliberals, even for the Post-War period, but they are generally considred as the figures who ushered in unprecedented waves of deregulation in the Post-War era.
